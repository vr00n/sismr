Our Story

A Staten Island Tradition Since 1956

The Staten Island Society of Model Railroaders, Inc. is one of Staten Island's longest-standing and most beloved hobby clubs.

First chartered in 1956, our club has called several Staten Island locations home — from our origins in Pleasant Plains, to Historic Richmond Town, to over a decade at Sea View Hospital Rehabilitation Center.

Our members combine craft, imagination, and engineering to recreate miniature worlds showcasing models of actual American trains. Using the popular HO-scale (1:87 ratio), each member builds their own module — a 2×4-foot section of railroad — that connects with others to form sprawling, detailed layouts.

“This is a hobby that lends itself to always being worked on. You can always add to it or subtract as you get new ideas and more products come on the market.”

— Tom Nolan, Past President

What We Do

Something for Everyone

Model railroading is more than a hobby — it's art, engineering, history, and community rolled into one.

Build & Operate Layouts

Members build individual modules (2×4 ft, HO-scale) that connect to form massive, detailed railroad layouts with lifelike scenery, bridges, towns, and rolling stock.

Community Shows

We set up free model railroad displays at schools, churches, and cultural institutions across Staten Island — sharing our passion and bringing smiles to kids and families.

Holiday Train Shows

Our annual Holiday Train Show is a beloved Staten Island tradition, drawing families from across the borough to marvel at miniature worlds in motion.

Buy, Sell & Trade

Members participate in buy-and-sell events across the tri-state area, finding rare pieces and sharing treasures with fellow enthusiasts in New Jersey and beyond.

Workshop & Learning

From beginners running their first set to experts building trains from scratch, we share skills in scenery, wiring, weathering, and scratch-building.

Fellowship

More than trains — it's the friendships. Members meet regularly to work together, share stories, and enjoy a creative hobby that spans generations.
